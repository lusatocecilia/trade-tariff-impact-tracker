# Trade & Tariff Impact Tracker: Automotive & Steel Sectors

## 📈 Project Overview
This project tracks and visualizes the relationship between shifting U.S. tariff rates and the resulting import/export volumes of automotive parts and raw steel. By examining time-series data, this tracker highlights how supply chains adjust to economic policy interventions.

## 🗂️ Data Sources
* **Data:** U.S. Census Bureau International Trade Data & FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data).
* **Timeframe:** 2018 – Present (Monthly intervals).

## 🔍 Key Insights & Methodology
* **Tariff Shock Realization:** Imports of structural steel saw a temporary contraction immediately following tariff implementations, followed by a price stabilization period as domestic suppliers filled market gaps.
* **Elasticity of Demand:** The automotive parts sector demonstrated highly inelastic behavior, as complex cross-border manufacturing networks made shifting production lines overseas costly.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
* **Data Gathering:** Excel / US Census Bureau API
* **Documentation & Presentation:** GitHub Markdown
